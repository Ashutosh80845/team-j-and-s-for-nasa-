SASSIE Arctic Field Campaign – Fall 2022

The SASSIE project (Salinity and Stratification at the Sea Ice Edge) is a NASA experiment to understand how melting sea ice affects the ocean, including sea surface temperature (SST), stratification, and future ice growth. From August to October 2022 the team sampled the transition from summer melt to autumn ice formation in the Beaufort Sea.

Measurements

  • Delta-18O measurements (ratio of 18O to 16O) to trace water sources.
  • Sea surface temperature (SST) and stratification observations.

Sample Collection

  • Water samples: GoFlo bottle lowered from the ship or Salinity Snake outflow.
  • Ice samples: Collected at ice stations by drilling and melting pieces for analysis.
Data format: netCDF • Campaign: Aug–Oct 2022

ECOSTRESS Land Surface Temperature & Emissivity (LST&E) – Public Data Summary

What It Is: ECOSTRESS is a sensor on the ISS that measures plant temperatures to see how much water plants need and how stressed they are.

Where It Works: Covers almost the whole world between 52°N and 52°S.

Available Data

  • ECO2LSTE Version 1 (now retired) measured land temperatures and emissivity in five thermal bands at 70 m resolution.
  • Data used a smart physics algorithm to separate temperature and emissivity.
  • You need ECO1BGEO to map it to real-world locations.

What’s Included

  • Temperature, emissivity for bands 1–5, quality checks, error info, wideband emissivity, and water vapor.

Past Issues

  • Some gaps in 2018–2019 due to storage problems.
  • 2019–2023: Only 3 bands collected sometimes; missing data replaced with placeholders.
  • Feb 2020: Band 4 had errors; Feb 17 had no data. All fixed now.
  • April 2023: Back to full 5-band mode; 3- and 5-band data are mixed for better coverage.
Source: ECOSTRESS • Bands: 1–5 • Coverage: ~52°N to 52°S

Deforestation

Deforestation in the Amazon: NASA's Predictions (2002-2050)

The dataset shows predicted deforestation in the Amazon Basin (includes Legal Amazon in Brazil + Guiana region + Amazon River watershed) from 2002 to 2050. Model used: SimAmazonia, which simulates monthly forest loss.

Two Scenarios

  1. Business-as-Usual (BAU): Uses historical deforestation trends (1997-2002), adds effects of paving major roads, and shows what happens if humans keep cutting forests at the same rate.
  2. Governance Scenario: Limits deforested land to 50% per subregion and includes protected areas to help slow deforestation; shows what happens if humans actively protect the forest.

Data Provided

  • 98 GeoTiff files: one map per year for each scenario (2002-2050).
  • 1 CSV file: contains the input data derived from satellite images.
Model: SimAmazonia • Years: 2002–2050
